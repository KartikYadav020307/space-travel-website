Moon

Chandrayaan-1 was India's first lunar probe. It was launched by the ISRO on 22 October 2008, and operated until August 2009. While, Chandrayaan-2 was launched from the second launch pad at SDSC on 22 July 2019 to the Moon by GSLV Mk III.

Mars

Mars Orbiter Mission (MOM), also called Mangalyaan, is a spacecraft orbiting Mars since 24 September 2014. It was launched on 5 November 2013 by ISRO. It was India's first interplanetary mission and ISRO has become the fourth space agency to reach Mars. The orbiter was finally called off on 2nd October, 2022.

astrosat

ASTROSAT was the first dedicated Indian Astronomy satellite mission launched by ISRO on 28 September 2015, which enabled multi-wavelength observations of the celestial bodies and cosmic sources in X-ray and UV spectral bands simultaneously.

Aditya-L1

Aditya-L1 is the first Indian observatory class mission to study the solar corona using a solar coronagraph. X-ray spectroscopic instruments will provide flare spectra while the in-situ payload observes the solar events. Aditya-L1 is expected to be launched in February 2023.

Avg. distance

148.18 Million km
